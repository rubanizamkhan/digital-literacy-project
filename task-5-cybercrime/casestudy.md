### **Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack**



Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It is one of the most common and dangerous forms of online fraud.



In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email, SMS, or message that appears to come from a legitimate organization such as a bank, e-commerce website, or social media platform. The message often creates urgency by stating that the account will be blocked or that immediate action is required. It usually contains a link that directs the user to a fake website designed to look exactly like the original one.



When the user enters their login credentials or financial details on this fake website, the information is instantly captured by the attacker. In some cases, attackers may also ask for OTPs to complete unauthorized transactions.



Students and young internet users are common targets because they frequently use online services but may not always verify the authenticity of links and messages. The consequences of phishing attacks can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.



With the rapid growth of digital platforms and online transactions, phishing attacks are increasing significantly. Therefore, awareness, alertness, and safe online practices are essential to protect oneself from such cybercrimes.
